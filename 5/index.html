02 — Studio

A studio of the inner Terai.

Flat land, big rivers, three wet months — the climate writes the first lines of every brief.

Founded in Bharatpur
Working across Chitwan
and the Terai

We are architects, designers and site people working from Narayangarh, Bharatpur. The inner Terai is subtropical floodplain, wet for a quarter of the year, and it rewards specific moves: raised plinths, deep eaves, shaded verandas, courtyards that ventilate, masonry that holds the night’s cool into the afternoon.

Our references are local before they are global. The Tharu longhouse — mud-plastered lattice on a floor lifted clear of the flood — taught this valley how to build long before any of us drew a line. The brick courtyards and carved lattice windows of the Newar towns are the country’s other great lesson in climate and craft. We design in conversation with both, alongside current structural practice and the municipal code.

  • 01

    Material

    Fired brick from Chitwan kilns, sal and sisau timber, lime and mud plasters, stone from the Rapti bed. Concrete where the structure asks for it — never as a finish.

  • 02

    Climate

    Openings sized for the low December sun and shaded from the high June one. Cross-ventilation is drawn before a single compressor is.

  • 03

    Water

    The plinth height of every project is set by the Narayani’s record years, not its average ones.

  • 04

    Craft

    Local masons and carpenters join from the first site visit; details are drawn for the hands that will build them.

03 — Process

From a client’s idea to a built site.

One studio, accountable for the whole arc. Most projects run eight to eighteen months from brief to handover.

  1. 01

    Ground & brief

    We start where the building will stand: levels, soil, water table, the neighbours’ shade, the road’s noise. The brief is written together, on site where possible.

  2. 02

    Design

    Sketch plans, a massing model, then developed drawings. You watch the courtyard, the light and the costs take shape at every review.

  3. 03

    Drawings & approvals

    Municipal submission drawings, structural design and building approvals through Bharatpur Metropolitan City — handled in-house.

  4. 04

    Cost & contract

    Bills of quantities, contractor selection, and a contract that names its rates before the first brick is ordered.

  5. 05

    Site

    We stay through construction — checking brick coursing, formwork, plaster samples and joinery until the keys change hands.
