CELL B.02 — TEXT

The studio works raw and minimal where the Terai is hottest and wettest. Architecture here is shaped by climate, connectivity and the quiet logic of the landscape — built with minimal intervention, its comfort drawn from light, materials, proportion and airflow. What a site already holds is integrated, not erased: at Bagar the riverbank stayed a riverbank, river stones in the plinth, raw log columns carrying hand-sculpted capitals.

A project runs as one unbroken line through this office — survey, sketch, municipal naksa drawings, structural coordination to NBC 105, tender, then weekly site supervision until handover. The client who arrives with an idea leaves with a building, and the same hands hold the file from first meeting to final defect list.

Every plate on this page is a real project of the studio — built work shot on site, design and concept work shown as studio renders.
