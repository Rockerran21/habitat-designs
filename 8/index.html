Bagar Restaurant

Restaurant · Rapti riverbank, Sauraha · Built 2024

Bagar is Nepali for the gravel bank a river leaves behind. The restaurant keeps the Rapti's bank as it was found — terraces follow the existing grade, raw log columns carry the roof beneath hand-sculpted capitals, and river stone is set straight into the plinth. The architecture arrives second; the site still reads as riverbank first.

The practice, in one line
We add the least the site will allow.

01

Climate first

Orientation, shade and airflow do the work before any machine does. Comfort is designed, not installed.

02

Local material

River stone, sal timber, brick and lime — sourced within reach of the site and left to read as themselves.

03

The site stays

Grade, trees and water are kept in the drawing. We build around what is already there, not over it.

The practice

Est. Bharatpur

Habitat Design Studio is a small architecture practice working across the Chitwan Valley — for private clients, hospitality operators and small institutions, from first sketch to site.

The studio is deliberately small, so the people who walk the site are the people who draw it. Each project begins on the ground, in its weather, and the brief is kept to what the place can carry. The result is buildings that feel inevitable — raw, minimal, and at ease where they stand.
