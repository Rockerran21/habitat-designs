Studio

02

माटो māto — earth, clay. The first material we measure on any site.

We work in the inner Terai: 38-degree May afternoons, two metres of rain between June and September, winter fog that sits low on the rivers.

Our buildings answer with mass and shade — thick masonry that carries the night's coolness through the day, verandas deep enough to live in, plinths raised above the flood marks on the neighbours' walls, openings placed for the through-draught rather than the street.

The reference points are local. The raised Tharu longhouse, with its mud-plastered lattice walls, taught this floodplain how to build long before we did; Newar joinery taught the country what carved restraint looks like. We take their logic and leave their ornament where it belongs — with the craftspeople who own it.

A drawing leaves this office only when it can be built by the contractors of Chitwan, with materials that arrive by road through Narayangarh: fired brick, sal timber, lime, and concrete used sparingly and shown plainly.

From Idea to Built Site

03
  1. 01

    Site & Brief

    Two visits before any drawing: sun path, the flood line on the neighbours' walls, where the wind enters in May, what the municipality will permit.

  2. 02

    Design

    Sketch to developed design in plans and sections, every room tested against light and the monsoon. We present buildings, not moods.

  3. 03

    Documentation

    Working drawings, structural coordination, and approval through Bharatpur Metropolitan City — carried by us, not left to the client.

  4. 04

    Tender & Contract

    The drawings priced with contractors we have built with before, and held to the details — joint by joint, course by course.

  5. 05

    Site to Handover

    Weekly supervision until the scaffolding comes down. The drawing set is finished only when the building is.

The same hands hold the project from the first conversation to the last coat of lime. That is the whole method.
