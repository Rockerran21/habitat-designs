The Studio

Bharatpur, Chitwan — inner Terai

A drafting room two streets from Pulchowk, working between the Narayani and the Rapti.

We work where the Terai is flattest and the rivers are widest. A building here must shed close to two metres of monsoon rain, hold its cool through forty-degree Aprils, and open to the river breeze when October finally clears the air. We design in that order: deep eaves first, cross-ventilation second, ornament last — architecture shaped by climate, connectivity, and the quiet logic of the landscape.

The drawings borrow from what has already proven itself on this floodplain — the raised floor of the Tharu longhouse, the shaded aangan at the centre of the plan, sal timber and brick fired within thirty kilometres of the site. Every project is measured against the same three lines: the floodline, the sun path, and the budget written on page one.

Habitat Design Studio takes a commission from the first site walk through municipal drawings, tender, and fortnightly supervision, to the day the client is handed the keys and the as-built set. One studio, one set of drawings, one responsible signature.

Drawing Register

Six phases, issued in sequence

How an idea becomes a building. Each phase closes with a document the client can hold, read, and question.

  1. Site & briefIssued: survey + brief note

    We walk the plot at midday and again at dusk. Sun, floodline, water table, and the neighbours' walls are recorded before any sketch is made.

  2. ConceptIssued: plans, sections, massing

    The courtyard is tested against the June sun path and the January fog. Two schemes, drawn to the same scale, argued over the same table.

  3. Drawing setIssued: municipal + working drawings

    Submission drawings, structural coordination, and a materials schedule priced in Narayangarh — not in a catalogue.

  4. Tender & contractIssued: BOQ + agreement

    We shortlist contractors whose work we have measured before. The bill of quantities is itemised down to the door ironmongery.

  5. Site supervisionIssued: site book, fortnightly

    Fortnightly visits, wet-season checks on damp-proofing and roof falls, and a site book written so the client can read it.

  6. HandoverIssued: as-built set + calendar

    As-built drawings, a maintenance calendar keyed to the monsoon, and one visit a year later to see what the building has learned.
