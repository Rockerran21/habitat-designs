Habitat Design Studio

We build from what the land already owns — the river's gravel, the sal timber, the slope it gives us to stand on.

Architecture shaped by climate, connectivity, and the quiet logic of the landscape; built with minimal intervention, so the comfort comes from light, materials, proportion, and airflow — not from fighting the place.

Selected work

Four projects · two built, two on the boards

Private residence·Chitwan·In design

NP Residence

A house turned inward around a courtyard: a perforated timber jaali screen filters the afternoon glare, arched plaster niches hold the shade, and a skylight drops a single column of light onto the internal garden. Master and three bedrooms gather around a common lobby, dining, and veranda.

Restaurant·Riverside Chitwan·Built

Bagar Restaurant

Built on the gravel bar the river left behind — bagar. The existing topography stays; we set raw log columns with hand-sculpted organic capitals onto a plinth of river stones, and let the site keep looking like itself. Minimal intervention, read literally.

View the work
Farmstay·Chitwan·Built

Himalchuli Nature Farmstay

Tiered clay-tile roofs step down into the sal canopy until the building all but disappears. From the approach you read the trees first and the architecture second — which is the point. The forest was here before the plan, and stays the larger fact.

View the work
Residence·Chitwan·In design

House 02

Bold geometry and layered volumes — a deliberate dialogue between straight and diagonal forms held around a central courtyard. Warm timber ceilings, slat screens, and a stone feature wall keep the heavy massing soft where the hand touches it.

Where the studio works

The inner Terai, between two rivers

We work the floodplain of Chitwan, where the Narayani and the Rapti set the rules — the floodline first, the sun path second, the budget on page one.

  • Narayani & Rapti rivers
  • Bharatpur — the studio

Four hands on every project

No single signature stands above the others. A building gets made when four positions agree — and the first of them is never the architect.

Principal

One responsible line

From the first site walk to the as-built set — one studio, one drawing set, one signature accountable for the whole.

Site

The river decides first

The floodline, the slope, the trees already standing. We measure what is there before we draw what is not, and we listen.

Craft

Made within thirty kilometres

Sal timber, river stone, brick fired nearby; log columns carved by hand. The detail is priced in Narayangarh, not a catalogue.

Client

A brief you can hold

Every phase closes with a document you can read and question — plans, a costed bill, a site book written in plain language.
