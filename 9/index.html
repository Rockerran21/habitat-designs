how we work

Bagar is the Nepali word for the gravel bar a river leaves behind. We were asked to build a restaurant on one, on the bank of the Rapti — so the topography stayed.

The studio works the same way on every site, whether riverbank, forest edge or courtyard: read what is already there — the grade, the stones, the trees, the way the air moves — and add only what the place asks for. Comfort comes from light, materials and proportion before anything mechanical. We work with private clients, hospitality operators and small institutions across the Chitwan Valley.

  1. 01VisitStand on the site, in its weather, before drawing a line.
  2. 02Measure what existsGrade, water, trees, stone, sun and prevailing wind — recorded as found.
  3. 03Draw the leastAdd the smallest intervention the site will allow, and stop.
